Flocculant, also known as pool clarifier, is a chemical substance that is used to clear cloudy water in swimming pools. It works by binding together tiny particles that are too small to be filtered out, making them easier to remove. Many pool owners are familiar with using flocculant, but have questioned whether it will still work under a solar blanket. In this article, we will explore the effectiveness of flocculant under a solar blanket and provide some tips on how to achieve the best results. First, let's understand the purpose of a solar blanket. A solar blanket is a large sheet of plastic that is placed on the surface of the pool to trap heat from the sun. This helps to keep the pool water warm, reduce evaporation, and save on heating costs. However, one of the downsides of using a solar blanket is that it can create a barrier between the water and the air, making it difficult for chemicals to circulate and work effectively. Now, back to the question at hand - will flocculant work under a solar blanket? The answer is yes, but with some considerations. Flocculant is an effective solution for clearing cloudy water, but it works best when the pool water is circulating. With a solar blanket in place, the circulation is reduced, and this can impact the effectiveness of flocculant. Here are some tips to ensure that flocculant works effectively under a solar blanket: 1. Run the pool pump - As mentioned earlier, flocculant works best when the pool water is circulating. Therefore, it is essential to run the pool pump for a few hours before adding flocculant. This will help to distribute the chemicals evenly throughout the pool. 2. Brush the pool walls and floor - Before adding flocculant, make sure to brush the pool walls and floor to loosen any debris or particles that may be stuck. This will help the flocculant to bind to these particles and make them easier to remove. 3. Add the flocculant in the evening - The best time to add flocculant is in the evening when the pool is not in use. This will allow the chemicals to work overnight without any disturbance from swimmers or the sun's UV rays. 4. Use the right amount of flocculant - It is crucial to follow the manufacturer's instructions when adding flocculant. Adding too little will not be effective, and adding too much can cause over-flocculation, resulting in a cloudy pool. It is recommended to use 1 ounce of flocculant per 5,000 gallons of water. 5. Vacuum the pool in the morning - After allowing the flocculant to work overnight, it is time to vacuum the pool in the morning. The particles that have been bound together by the flocculant will settle to the bottom of the pool, making it easier to vacuum them out. In conclusion, flocculant can still work effectively under a solar blanket, but it requires some extra attention and proper application. By following the tips mentioned above, you can achieve a crystal clear pool even with a solar blanket in place. Remember to also regularly clean and maintain your pool to prevent cloudy water from occurring in the first place. Happy swimming!
